What is a Pomodoro Clock?
A pomodoro clock is a clock invented by Francesco Cirillo in the late 1908s. It is a special type of time managment system in which a timer is used to separate work and break time. Normally, for every 25 minutes of work, 5 minutes of break time are awarded. This method of timeboxing is applied in such a way in order to increase productivity. Usually, after 25 minutes of work, the mind will be "tricked" into working further, thus enhancing productivity.
